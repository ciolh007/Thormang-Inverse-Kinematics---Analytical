Thormang robot has 8 joints in each arm including the gripper

Analytical solution only possible for two links/actuators. Two possible solutions

Reducing Dimensionality

Reduce the kinematic chain to a series of independent two link problems

Break the arm into two parts:

  1. Arm from shoulder to wrist
  2. Wrist to fingers (End effector)

Seperate Arm and End effector

If the wrist is too far away from the target, then it will be impossible to reach the target by moving the end effector

One idea is to move the arm to any location close to the target (coarse approach) and then to use the end effector to approach the target (fine approach)

Depending on the application we may prefer certain orientations between end effector and target. Can back calculate the position of the wrist from the position and orientation of the target

Same idea when dealing with humanoid robots walking - feet parallel to ground and parallel to direction of motion

Seperation of Arm and Wrist

Brake kinematic chain of the arm into two components: arm and wrist

Arm: 4 joints

Wrist: 4 joints

Arm - Reduction of Dimensionality

  1. J1: Shoulder Lateral: in home position, moves the arm in the lateral plane (Pitch)
  2. J2: Shoulder Frontal: in home position, moves arm in the frontal plane (Swing/Tilt)
  3. J3: Elbow Transveral: in home position, moves arm in the transversal plane (Yaw)
  4. J4: Elbow Lateral: in home position, moves arm in the lateral plane (Pitch)

J1 and J4 work together in the same plane

J2 is the only one that can move the arm in the Y direction

J3 does not affect the position of the wrist at all

Arm - Reduction of Dimensionality

So we can remove J3 from consideration to approach the target - only used to orient end effector. θ_arm_3 is arbitrary (0 deg.)

We calculate the angle between shoulder and target in the Y direction (X-Z plane). We can calculate θ_arm_2 from position or torso and target location

Once we know θ_2, then we can establish the plane from shoulder to target. Only J1 and J4 left - so we can solve that two-link system. Calculate θ_arm_1 and θ_arm_4

Wrist - Reduction of Dimensionality

Wrist is similar to head as its main purpose is to align (change the orientation)

Wrist orienatation is determined by three independent joints θ_wrist_1 (yaw), θ_wrist_2 (pitch), θ_wrist_3 (roll)

θ_wrist_4 is the gripper (open/closed) - depends on what we want to pick up (e.g., size of the cup)

Leg - Reduction of Dimensionality

Break into leg (hip to ankle - 4 joints) and foot parts (ankle to sole of the foot - 2 joints)

If feet parallel to the ground and parallel to the direction of motion, then we can back-calculate position of the ankle

Transversal orientation of feet can only be controlled by join 1 in the hip. Calculate θ_leg_1

Position in the y direction is controlled by joint 2 in the hip. Calculate θ_leg_2

Now we are left with a two link problem (joint 3 and joint 4). Calculate θ_leg_3 and θ_leg_4

Calculate angles for the ankle/foot to make sure the feet are: (a) parallel to the ground, and (b) parallel to the direction of motion. Calculate θ_leg_5 and θ_leg_6

Head Assembly

Adjust orientation relative to robot (pan and tilt)

Each join acts on one dimension independently

Calculate θ_head_1 and θ_head_2
